First Impressions

In our first week, I realized HCI is much bigger than just "making pretty screens." It’s actually a mix of psychology, computer science, and even sociology. One thing that stuck with me is that we don't just "use" computers; we interact with them as agents.

The "Aha!" Moment: Miller's Law

I used to think that every menu should only have 7 items because of "Miller's Law." But according to Dix et al. (p. 29), this is a total myth in UI design!

Miller's Law is about Working Memory—the stuff we have to hold in our heads. Since a menu is right there on the screen, we can recognize it rather than having to recall it. So, you don't have to limit your menus to 7 items!

How the "Human Processor" Works

We looked at how humans are like computers in a way. We have:

  • Sensory Input: Seeing the screen or hearing a notification.
  • Processing: Thinking about what to click (Cognitive Processor).
  • Output: Moving the mouse or tapping the screen (Motor Processor).

This "Sense-Decide-Act" loop is the heart of every interaction we design.

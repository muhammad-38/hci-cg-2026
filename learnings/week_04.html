GOMS: The "Expert" Model

Last week, Norman's model was about how users think and feel. This week, GOMS (Goals, Operators, Methods, Selection Rules) showed me a different side of HCI. It treats the user like a professional who already knows the system.

As we discussed in the Toggle Switch Interaction example (Slide 1), GOMS breaks down a simple "Turn on Wi-Fi" task into the specific logic an expert uses to choose the fastest route.

KLM: The Stopwatch of HCI

The Keystroke-Level Model (KLM) is like a stopwatch. By using the operators from Dix et al. (p. 437), we can predict task time.

  • K (Keystroke): ~0.2s
  • P (Pointing): ~1.1s
  • H (Homing): ~0.4s (Moving hand from mouse to keys)
  • M (Mental Preparation): ~1.35s (The "thinking" pause)

My Takeaway: Who Paid?

The "Who Paid" problem from our lab assignment (Slide 6) showed why we need these models. When a bill is split, the **Cognitive Load** is huge because users have to rely on **Recall**. By using KLM, we can see that a manual calculation takes way longer than a well-designed UI "toggle" because of all the 'M' (Mental) operators involved.
